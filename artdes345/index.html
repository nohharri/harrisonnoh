Hunt Statement

"We want to create a collaborative study space for students to improve communication, efficiency and accountability through conducting interviews."

Literature Review

Summary Findings: Benefits of Collaborative Learning

  • Productivity in groups increases, resulting in higher achievement and supportive environment
  • Shared artifacts
  • Collaboration Environments: handwritten, technology based, exclusive, private

Marjan Laal - Seyed Mohammad Ghodsi - Procedia - Social and Behavioral Sciences - 2012

Dillenbourg P. (1999) What do you mean by collaborative learning?. In P. Dillenbourg (Ed) Collaborative-learning: Cognitive and Computational Approaches. (pp.1-19). Oxford: Elsevier

Stahl, G., Koschmann, T., & Suthers, D. (2006). Computer-supported collaborative learning: An historical perspective. In R. K. Sawyer (Ed.), Cambridge handbook of the learning sciences (pp. 409-426). Cambridge, UK: Cambridge University Press.

Research Summary

  • Interviewed 7 College Students
  • Majors ranged from Business School, Psychology, Engineering, Organizational studies (etc).
  • All University of Michigan Students (Ages 20-24)
  • Roommates/ friends/ classmates with majors that require both individual and group work
  • Asked to participate in project research

Interview Findings

  • Sight/Noise
  • Interaction
  • Availability of resources
  • Security
  • Space matching purpose
  • Accessibility
  • Environment
  • Most students when working in a group setting...
  • Consider the environment to study in - Ex. If they need a laptop, the environment must have outlets.
  • Have a problem attempting to get everyone on the same page, especially with missing group members.
  • Tend to get distracted easily by social media / texting, especially since working on individual laptops
  • Since working on individual laptops it is difficult for everyone to crowd around a singular computer and help one another

Accountability

Making sure that everyone is living up to their responsibilities and expectations

Efficiency

Making sure that no one is distracting themselves with social media and messaging

Communication

With busy and conflicting schedules, people find it difficult to catch everyone up on the same page

Concept Generation

  • Recreating Natural Lighting
  • Health/Security Services
  • Multi-Function Rooms
  • Artifical Waterfall
  • Scheduling Tablet

Narrowing it Down

  • Interactive Table
  • Various features to address interviewee’s issues
  • Audio Recording
  • Recap file
  • Sharing screen
  • Checklist
  • Ambient lighting to monitor progress
  • Lighting display
  • Pressure sensitive
  • Form reservations

Concept Generation

  1. To ease the workflow as well as minimize distractions and disruptions
  2. People in group projects tend to lack responsibilities, so there is a desire to make sure that everyone is on the same page and knows exactly what is expected of them.
  3. Provide accurate descriptions of what was covered for missing group member(s) and a need to reference something that was covered in the meeting time if someone forgot or did not write it down.
